Sinchan Sinha Project Case Study
Project 02 · Performance Marketing

Programmatic Display Campaign Optimization Analysis

Period: February – March 2025 Domain: Paid Channel Analytics · Attribution Tools: Excel · Google Display Network
Section 01

Business Problem

Digital advertising is expensive, and the margin for inefficiency is thin. For marketing teams running programmatic display campaigns, the challenge is not just spending the budget — it is spending it well.

Without proper analysis, campaign teams are flying blind. They know their ads are running, but they cannot clearly explain which targeting decisions are driving results, which placements are wasting money, and what adjustments would actually move the needle on key metrics.

"Without a structured analytical process, answers were either unavailable or buried in raw data exports that required hours of manual work to interpret — resulting in slow optimization cycles and delayed decisions."

The core business questions driving this project:

  • Is the current cost-per-acquisition (CPA) acceptable, and where is it highest?
  • Which targeting parameters and audience segments are generating the best click-through rates?
  • How should the budget be reallocated to maximize performance across placements?
  • How do daily performance trends shift, and what does that mean for optimization timing?
Section 02

Solution Approach

A simulated Google Display Network campaign was constructed with realistic performance data — daily impressions, clicks, conversions, spend, and audience-level breakdowns. This environment allowed the full analytical process to be developed and validated against data that mirrors real-world campaign complexity.

The analysis centered on three core metrics tracked daily: CTR (click-through rate — how engaging the ad and targeting are), CPM (cost per thousand impressions — how efficiently budget is deployed for reach), and CPA (cost per acquisition — the most critical metric for campaign efficiency). Daily tracking made it possible to catch performance shifts early before inefficiencies compounded.

Trend analysis was applied to identify patterns across time — not just average performance, but when performance peaked, when it dipped, and what campaign variables correlated with those movements. This temporal analysis is what separates reactive management from proactive optimization.

Attribution-style analysis was layered on top: evaluating which targeting parameters, audience segments, and placement types were most consistently associated with conversion outcomes — providing the data basis for confident budget reallocation decisions.

The entire workflow was housed in a dynamic Excel dashboard with pivot tables, conditional formatting, and dynamic charting — designed to update automatically as new performance data was entered, eliminating manual chart rebuilds after each reporting cycle.

Section 03

Results & Business Impact

↓hrs

A 20% improvement in CTR through targeted optimization of audience parameters and creative-placement matching is significant in programmatic advertising — even incremental CTR improvements translate directly into better quality scores, lower costs, and stronger overall campaign economics.

The 18% CPA reduction came from budget reallocation away from underperforming segments toward placements with stronger conversion signals. In a live campaign context, this directly reduces the cost of acquiring each customer — compounding in value as scale increases.

The dynamic dashboard eliminated the manual reporting burden that typically consumes hours of a media analyst's week, freeing capacity for strategic optimization rather than data preparation. The methodology is directly transferable to live campaigns across any programmatic platform.
