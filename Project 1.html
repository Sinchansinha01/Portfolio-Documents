Sinchan Sinha Project Case Study
Project 01 · Revenue Operations

SaaS Revenue & Funnel Analytics Dashboard

Period: March – April 2025 Domain: GTM Analytics · Revenue Operations Tools: SQL · Power BI · DAX
Section 01

Business Problem

For any SaaS company, the pipeline is the lifeblood of the business. But knowing your pipeline exists and actually understanding what is happening inside it are two very different things.

This project was born out of a challenge that GTM and Revenue Operations teams face constantly: leadership could see top-line numbers in their CRM, but they had no visibility into why deals were converting — or more importantly, why they were not. The marketing team was generating leads, the sales team was working those leads, but nobody had a single, clean view that tied these pieces together.

"Without a centralized performance view, resource planning relied on gut feel rather than evidence. Marketing budgets were hard to justify, and sales leaders lacked the data to identify where the real bottlenecks lived."

Key questions the business needed to answer:

  • Which lead sources were producing the highest-quality pipeline?
  • Where in the funnel were deals stalling or dropping off?
  • Was the team on track to hit quarterly revenue targets?
  • Which customer segments represented the strongest lifetime value?
Section 02

Solution Approach

The approach started with the data itself. Raw deal and lead data was extracted and cleaned using SQL — a querying language used to organize and manipulate data stored in databases. The dataset contained inconsistencies common to CRM exports: duplicate records, missing close dates, inconsistent lead source labels, and pipeline stages that didn't always match the actual sales process. These were resolved before any analysis began.

Once clean, SQL queries were written to calculate conversion rates at each funnel stage — from initial lead capture, through qualified opportunities, to closed-won deals. This stage-by-stage view made it possible to pinpoint exactly where leads were getting stuck in the pipeline.

Revenue forecasting models were layered on top. By analyzing historical deal velocity — how long deals typically spent at each stage — and combining that with the current open pipeline value, the dashboard could project likely revenue outcomes for the quarter. This gave leadership a data-backed forecast, not just a number a sales rep submitted manually.

Customer segmentation was incorporated to add strategic depth. Customers were grouped by industry, deal size, and acquisition channel — revealing which segments converted fastest, generated the highest average contract value, and showed the strongest retention signals.

All of this was brought to life in Power BI, using DAX measures for dynamic calculations. The dashboard was designed for two audiences: executives who needed a high-level pipeline health summary, and analysts who needed drill-down capability into specific segments or time periods.

Section 03

Results & Business Impact

The completed dashboard delivered a single source of truth for GTM performance — something that had not existed before. Where leadership previously pieced together insights from multiple disconnected reports, they could now access a real-time view of the entire revenue engine in one place.

The funnel visibility made it immediately clear which stages were underperforming. Rather than reviewing generic win/loss ratios, the team could see the precise points where deal momentum broke down — enabling targeted coaching and smarter resource allocation.

The segmentation analysis added strategic value beyond reporting. By identifying which customer profiles generated the best outcomes, the business had a clearer basis for refining its ideal customer profile and realigning marketing investment accordingly.
